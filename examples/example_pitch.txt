# Shape of Thought — Pitch

Shape of Thought is an AI-powered learning tool that turns any lecture or course
material into a personalized, interactive concept map — helping students (especially
those with ADHD or attention difficulties) build genuine understanding instead of
passive note-taking.

## The Problem
Traditional lectures are one-size-fits-all. Students with ADHD, who represent roughly
16% of college students, struggle to maintain focus and build mental models from
linear lecture formats. The average attention span in a lecture drops to 10 minutes,
and students routinely lose the "conceptual thread" even when they're physically
present. This translates to roughly a half-grade-point deficit compared to their
non-ADHD peers in standard lecture courses.

## The Solution
Shape of Thought uses a three-stage AI pipeline:
1. **Extraction**: Course materials (slides, transcripts, readings) are processed to
   identify core concepts and relationships.
2. **Mapping**: A hierarchical concept map is generated, with connections between
   ideas made explicit and color-coded by type (causal, definitional, sequential).
3. **Interaction**: Students navigate the map interactively — zooming in for detail,
   clicking to surface examples, and asking questions that are answered in context.

The educator reviews and approves the map before it's shared with students, ensuring
accuracy and pedagogical alignment.

## Traction
- Pilot with 3 Calc 1A sections at Foothill College (~90 students)
- 83% of pilot students reported the map helped them follow the lecture better
- Average quiz scores 12% higher in map-assisted sections vs. control
